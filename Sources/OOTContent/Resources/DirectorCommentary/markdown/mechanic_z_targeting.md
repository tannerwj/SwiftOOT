When Z-targeting kicks in, OoT stops solving combat and camera separately.

- Combat picks a lock-on candidate and stores a shared target snapshot.
- Movement pivots around that target instead of pure stick direction.
- Camera framing also changes so the player, target, and readable attack space all stay in frame together.

That is why lock-on feels so foundational to 3D Zelda: it is a coordination layer for several systems at once, not just a camera trick.
